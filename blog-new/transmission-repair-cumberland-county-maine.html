Warning Signs Your Transmission Needs Attention

Transmissions give you clues before they fail completely. Pay attention to these:

  • Slipping gears — The engine revs but the car doesn't accelerate the way it should. Feels like the power disappears for a second, then catches. Common when pulling onto Route 302 from a side road and you need to merge quickly.
  • Delayed or hard shifting — A noticeable pause or jolt when shifting between gears. Automatic transmissions should shift smoothly — if you can feel every gear change, something's off.
  • Grinding or shaking — In a manual transmission, grinding when shifting usually means clutch or synchronizer issues. In an automatic, shaking or shuddering during shifts can indicate worn clutch packs or torque converter problems.
  • Transmission fluid leak — Bright red or dark brownish-red fluid pooling under your car. Check your driveway or garage floor. Transmission fluid is critical — run it low and you'll cause serious internal damage fast.
  • Burning smell — Overheated transmission fluid has a distinct, acrid smell. If you notice it after driving, especially after towing or climbing hills, your transmission is running too hot.
  • Check engine or transmission warning light — Modern vehicles monitor transmission performance through sensors. A warning light means the computer detected abnormal behavior. Get it scanned.
  • Won't engage or go into gear — If you shift into drive or reverse and nothing happens, this is urgent. Don't force it — call a shop.

The bottom line: Every one of these symptoms gets worse and more expensive with time. A $200 fluid service today can prevent a $3,500 rebuild next month. Don't wait.

Types of Transmission Service

Not all transmission work is the same. Here's what we handle at our shop, from routine maintenance to major repair:

  • Fluid change or flush — Fresh fluid keeps internal components lubricated and cooled. This is the most affordable way to protect your transmission.
  • Solenoid replacement — Electronic solenoids control fluid flow. A failing one causes hard shifts or slipping. Often fixable without pulling the transmission.
  • Clutch replacement (manual) — If you drive a stick, the clutch is a wear item. Slipping under load or chattering from a stop means it's time.
  • Torque converter service — Connects engine to transmission in automatics. Shudder or overheating can point here.
  • Full rebuild or replacement — When internal damage is extensive, the transmission comes out for a rebuild or swap with a remanufactured unit.

We provide all of these transmission services and always start with the least invasive approach. We don't assume the worst — we verify.
