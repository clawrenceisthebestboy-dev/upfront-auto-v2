Why Tire Rotation Actually Matters

Most people treat tire rotation as optional. It isn't. Here's why:

  • Front tires wear faster — they handle steering and most of the braking load. Without rotation, you'll replace fronts 30–40% sooner than rears
  • Even wear = better handling — mismatched tread depth across axles affects how your car responds, especially on wet roads
  • Extends total tire life by 20–30% — tires aren't cheap. A $25 rotation saves you hundreds
  • Catches problems early — while we rotate, we're looking at brake wear, suspension components, and any unusual wear patterns that signal alignment or pressure issues

Rotation Patterns — We Use the Right One for Your Car

Not all rotations are the same. Front-wheel drive, rear-wheel drive, and AWD each have different optimal rotation patterns. Directional tires have their own requirements. We know the difference and do it right — not just move all four corners randomly.

Signs You're Overdue for a Rotation

  • Vibration in the steering wheel at highway speeds
  • Car pulls to one side on a straight road
  • Uneven tread depth when you check front vs. rear tires
  • More than 7,500 miles since your last rotation
  • You can't remember the last time you had it done
