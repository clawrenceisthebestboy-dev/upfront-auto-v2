Signs Your Transmission Needs Attention

Don't ignore these — they almost always get worse:

  • Slipping gears — engine revs up but the car doesn't accelerate, or it unexpectedly pops out of gear
  • Delayed engagement — you shift into Drive or Reverse and there's a pause before it moves
  • Grinding or shaking — especially when shifting, often means worn gear synchronizers
  • Transmission fluid leak — red or brownish fluid under your car near the center or front
  • Burning smell — overheating transmission fluid smells distinct and sharp
  • Check engine light — many transmission codes trigger the CEL before you feel any symptoms
  • Noisy in neutral — bumping or whining sounds when the car is in park or neutral
⚠️ Don't keep driving on a slipping transmission. What starts as a soft slip can quickly damage torque converters, clutch packs, and valve bodies — turning a minor repair into a full rebuild.

Automatic vs. Manual Transmission Repairs

We service both. Automatic transmissions are more common in modern vehicles and involve fluid systems, solenoids, and complex electronic controls. Manual transmissions have fewer electronic components but require precise clutch and synchronizer work. Either way, the first step is accurate diagnosis — not guessing.
