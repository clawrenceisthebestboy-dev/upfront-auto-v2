Common Truck Problems We See in Maine

Rust and corrosion. Maine road salt is relentless. We see rust on brake lines, exhaust systems, frame components, and undercarriage on trucks all the time. Early detection matters — a rusted brake line is a cheap fix compared to a compromised frame.

Ball joints and tie rods. Trucks carry heavier loads and take harder hits from potholes. Ball joint and tie rod wear is constant on F-150s, RAMs, and Silverados in this region.

Wheel bearings. Heavy trucks, rough roads, and Maine winters combine to accelerate wheel bearing wear. A grinding noise from a wheel that changes pitch when you turn is usually a bearing.

Transfer case and 4WD issues. Maine 4WD gets used hard in winter. Transfer case fluid service and actuator issues are common, especially on trucks past 100K miles.

Exhaust rust-through. The bottom of your exhaust is always wet in a Maine winter. Mufflers, pipes, and hangers corrode faster here than anywhere else in the country.

Suspension wear. Struts, shocks, and leaf springs on trucks wear faster when carrying loads or towing. If your truck squats or bounces more than it used to, it's time for an inspection.

Maine is hard on trucks. Salt, potholes, and heavy use accelerate wear on every system. Regular inspections catch small problems before they become big bills.
